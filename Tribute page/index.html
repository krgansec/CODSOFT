Tribute to Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh was a revolutionary freedom fighter and a prominent figure in the Indian independence movement. Born on September 28, 1907, in Banga, Punjab, he played a crucial role in the fight against British colonial rule.

Bhagat Singh is best known for his acts of protest against oppressive British policies. His courage and sacrifice, particularly in the Lahore Conspiracy Case and the hunger strike in jail, made him a symbol of resistance.

On March 23, 1931, Bhagat Singh was executed by the British colonial rulers. Even in the face of death, he remained steadfast in his commitment to the cause of independence.
